I was born and bred in Chicago, although grew up in the burbs so some might say I'm not actually from Chicago.
I lived in Des Plaines, LaGrange, Melrose Park, and currently in Hoffman Estates, so I've seen more of the area than most people.
I did my undergrad at Northern Illinois University in French Language and Literature. While not the smartest choice I've made, I can now communicate with people all over the world in three of the most spoken languages, so I've got that going for me.

Since I did not want to teach French, I just flowed to where life took me after graduation. I ended up in an office job doing basically data entry. It was the most exciting job you could ever think of, believe me. After deciding that wasn't for me, I chose nursing, as the medical field had always interested me. So I started taking gen-ed's for that, but part-time that alone would've taken me at least a year and a half, which seemed like a long time at the moment. I heard of coding bootcamps from someone close to me, did my research, and loved the idea of less time in school with more pay at the end of it. Less stress and bodily fluids too. Win-win. And now I'm here.
